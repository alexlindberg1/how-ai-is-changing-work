1. Project Overview

Bluewater Hotel & Conference Center has experienced recurring pinhole leaks in the original 1998 copper domestic water distribution system serving the east wing (floors 2–4, 48 guest rooms). Repair frequency has increased from two events in 2024 to seven events in the past twelve months, resulting in guest relocations, drywall repairs, and rising emergency service costs.

Summit Plumbing & Mechanical proposes a phased repipe of the east wing domestic water system using Type L copper mains and PEX-A branch distribution, executed floor-by-floor to keep the hotel operational throughout the project.

2. Scope of Work

  • Replace approximately 1,850 linear feet of domestic hot, cold, and recirculation piping serving the east wing.
  • Install new isolation valves at each floor and each guest-room group (12 zones total), allowing future repairs without wing-wide shutdowns.
  • Replace the east wing recirculation pump and balancing valves.
  • Re-insulate all new piping to current energy code.
  • Open and patch access points in corridor ceilings and select room walls; paint-ready finish by our licensed drywall subcontractor.
  • Pressure test and disinfect each zone prior to return to service.
  • Provide as-built riser diagrams and a valve schedule at close-out.

Exclusions: Fixture replacement, water heater plant, fire protection piping, hazardous material abatement (none expected; pre-1990 materials not present), and finish painting.

5. Assumptions

  • Hotel provides access to guest rooms per the agreed phasing schedule.
  • Existing shut-off valves at the mechanical room function adequately for tie-ins; allowance included for two valve replacements if required.
  • No asbestos-containing materials are present in work areas.
  • Corridor ceiling access panels may be reused where undamaged.
  • Guest room finishes beyond paint-ready patching are handled by hotel staff or others.
